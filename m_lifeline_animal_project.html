Lifeline Animal Project

#September 2020 #MobileApp #Nonprofit

LifeLine Animal Project is an Atlanta-based animal shelter leading the way to make Atlanta a no-kill zone for animals. However, this nonprofit struggles to communicate their services with the community. Our team designed a mobile app that connects interested community members with opportunities to volunteer, foster, or adopt at their local shelter.

I worked as the primary UI designer and designed the pet-matching feature which functions similarly to popular mobile dating apps.

User Research

Research Question
How do community members discover and interpret the resources offered by Lifeline Animal Project?

Methodology

  • 6 15-minute interviews with pertinent users, people who fostered or adopted a dog from an animal shelter in the last six months.
  • Online survey with 40 participants. The survey inquired about the motivation for pet ownership and adoption. (McKenzie)
  • Key stakeholder interview with Lifeline Animal Project to gain insight on how the animal shelter viewed their own goals and needs (Nikki).

Results

The Problem

Communication between the shelter and the Metro-Atlanta community is a consistent challenge. The animal shelter needs to create a long-lasting relationship with volunteers, foster parents, and potential pet owners in order to fulfill their mission.

Ideation

After user research, our team brainstormed useful features to improve communication between the shelter and the community. We found that matchmaking, placing the right animal with the right foster or adoption parent, is a neglected component in Lifeline Animal Project’s current communication practices.

"Mobile applications are convenient and easy-to-use!"

"Wouldn’t it be fun to swipe and match with your favorite furry friend?"

"Perhaps, a quick quiz to match the perfect pet with their lifestyle?"

"An in-app messaging feature would improve response time."
