About Me

My name is McKenzie Peterson. I am a UX Designer passionate about frequent user testing and intelligent design solutions. I have worked on mobile applications, web-based solutions, and responsive web redesigns.

In a recent website redesign, I worked in a team of four to improve the information architecture and visual branding of the Atlanta Film Series website. On a mobile application team, I designed a unique pet matching feature to meet the needs of future pet-owners.

I graduated from the University of Georgia with a Bachelor of Arts degree in History, a Studio Art Minor, and a Museum Studies Certificate in December of 2019. I specialized in public history with an emphasis on technology. My training as a historian makes me an analytical problem solver and writer. As the communications intern at the Georgia Museum of Art, I wrote concise press releases and interviewed curators about upcoming exhibitions. History and User Experience Design are not unrelated fields because both require extensive research and relentless curiosity.

I am also a runner, cyclist, and visual artist.
